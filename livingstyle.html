Effects Of Social Media

One of the most obvious negative aspects of social networking is losing face-to-face interactions with other people. Teens are sitting around with their gadgets in their hands all day long, tapping messages onto screens instead of communicating with real people. Many children feel more comfortable with virtual friends than with real ones.

The time teens are spending on texting and online communication means they are missing out on developing critical social skills. By communicating mostly through a media site, they are not learning how to read body language, facial expressions, or vocal inflections. They may not even be able to formulate a response in real time when interacting with people. Which also leads to the fact that they won't be able to convey thier feelings effectively. This creates communication gap thereby creating casees of irritaion,intolerance,lack of confidence and frustation of not able to convey one's thoughts.

Communicating through a screen also removes the more personal and intimidating aspects of human interaction. It's easier to keep your guard up with a text and you can't immediately see how your words are having an effect on someone. Some teens now say that talking over the phone is too intense because it is too direct.

Anxiety over the basic act of talking can lead to problems later in life as social negotiations become more complex with employment. Teen nowadays judge themselves on the basis on the likes they get. Nowadays for teens opinions of other people matter so much that it creates a tendency to present oneself as someone who will be appreciated by all. This leads to dissatisfaction and poor self image which ultimately leads to mental problems.
